### 3. Enhancing Lumity: Product Redesign for an Optimized Learning Experience
**Status:** Live on existing portfolio at `/project/lumity-redesign`
**Client:** Lumity
**Type:** UX Design, Product Redesign

**Tagline (existing):**
> "A user-centered redesign of the app's interface to enhance usability, visual consistency, and engagement."

**Project Details:**
- Team: Herself + 3
- Duration: 16 weeks
- Tools: Figma, FigJam, UXtweak, Useberry
- Skills: UX Design, Usability Testing, User Research, Systems Design, Product Architecture

**Platform description:**
> "Lumity is an information sharing platform to engage in positive, productive content with your like-minded, growth-oriented learning community."

**Project goal:**
> "This project involved the redesign of the Lumity application, with the goal to increase usability, improve consistency in design, and build user engagement through exploration and community features."

**The Challenge:**
> "Lumity is a platform for sharing learning journeys and building community around lifelong growth. However, the original app struggled with:
> - Fragmented navigation between personal learning and community features
> - Inconsistent visuals and interaction patterns
> - Overwhelming content interactions that hindered user engagement
> - A lack of unified spaces for note-taking, learning, and social connection"

**Central Question:**
> "How might we transform Lumity into a unified, engaging platform that seamlessly connects personal learning and community experiences?"

**The Solution:**
> "Redesign Lumity to:
> - Create seamless navigation between personal, exploratory, and community spaces
> - Establish a consistent visual and interaction system
> - Simplify complex workflows and reduce cognitive load
> - Encourage user engagement through clearer pathways and community features"

**Design Approach:**
> "An end-to-end UX process combined research, testing, and design:
> - Research & Insights: Explored user needs through interviews, ecosystem mapping, and competitive analysis
> - Design Strategy: Defined principles focused on clarity, consistency, and community engagement
> - Architecture Redesign: Simplified navigation and information structure for intuitive use
> - Prototyping & Testing: Built and tested prototypes to validate usability and refine designs
> - Design System: Developed scalable UI components for visual consistency and future growth"

**Key Research Finding:**
> "One of the most insightful takeaways came from creating the ecosystem map which highlighted that users exist in progressive levels, starting at the individual level, growing to explore the community, and then to actively contributing to the community."

**Three Key App Functions:**
1. Personal Tracking — tools for tracking progress and organizing content in Library
2. Exploring Interests — discovering new topics and specific content
3. Creating Community — actively engaging with others and contributing to community discussions

**Usability Testing (4 users, mid-fi prototype):**
- Media types looked too similar — users couldn't distinguish articles, books, podcasts, videos
- Tabs on Home and Explore were missed — overlooked or misunderstood
- Category-based Explore felt rigid — users wanted more flexible browsing

**Design Iterations:**
- Clearer media cues → distinct shapes and icons per media type
- Redesigned tabs → stronger visual weight + descriptive labels
- Dynamic Explore → masonry-style layout replacing rigid categories

**Final Prototype Features:**
- Onboarding & Homepage: "Set your interests fast and land on a personalized homepage built just for you"
- Explore: "Swipe through a dynamic Explore page with content organized by media type, tags, and community"
- Posting: "Save drafts, pick up where you left off, and add posts directly to your library"
- Library: "Switch between playlists or saved posts in grid or list view for full control"
- Progress and Identity: "Stay motivated with streaks, goals, and achievements that bring your learning journey to life"

**Key Lessons:**
> "What I learned most from this project was the value of testing early and often: small usability insights shaped some of the most impactful design changes."
> "Building a design system also reinforced the importance of scalability, ensuring the product could grow consistently beyond the prototype."
> "This project not only strengthened my skills in UX research and systems thinking, but also deepened my ability to tell a clear product story through design."

### 5. Type You Can Hear: Making Bungee Accessible through Cross-Sensory Design
**Status:** Live on existing portfolio at `/project/bungee-accessibility`
**Client:** Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum
**Type:** Accessibility Design, UX Research

**Tagline (existing):**
> "An accessible reimagining of Cooper Hewitt's Bungee font tester, designed to translate visual type into perceivable experiences for screenreader users."

**Project Details:**
- Team: Herself + 4
- Duration: 3 weeks
- Tools: Figma, FigJam, AI Voice Generation
- Skills: UX Design, Accessibility Design, User Research

**The Challenge:**
> "Typography testers like the Bungee Font Tester are highly visual experiences." Users couldn't experience Bungee's layering and color interactions, or its playful personality.

**Central Question:**
> "How can we ensure that Bungee's dynamic typographic personality remains meaningful and accessible to users who rely on visual description?"

**Research Stats:**
- 51.9M U.S. adults report some level of vision loss
- 307,000 U.S. adults are completely blind
- 2.2B people globally live with visual impairments

**The Solution:**
- Redesigned for screen readers
- Translated visual qualities (color, layering, weight, orientation) into sound
- Met WCAG 2.2 accessibility standards
- Simplified controls, fully visible interface, preset color options
- Keyboard-focusable elements + guided tutorial
